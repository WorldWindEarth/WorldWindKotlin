Ellipsoid Tutorial

Demonstrates how to add 3D Ellipsoid shapes to a RenderableLayer.

An Ellipsoid is a 3D volumetric shape with three independent semi-axes (xRadius / yRadius / zRadius). Setting xRadius == yRadius produces a rotation spheroid (the common "two-radius" case where one equatorial radius pairs with a polar radius). Lighting (Lambertian shading) is opt-in via attributes.isLightingEnabled.

This example renders six ellipsoids:

  • The Northwestern sphere is lit (Lambertian shading on).
  • The Northeastern sphere is unlit (flat fill) - same shape, for comparison.
  • The Western shape is an oblate spheroid (equatorial > polar).
  • The Eastern shape is a prolate spheroid (polar > equatorial).
  • The Southwestern shape is tri-axial with a 30 degree heading and a yellow outline.
  • The Southeastern shape is ground-clamped: its centre drops to the terrain so the sphere is half-submerged.
