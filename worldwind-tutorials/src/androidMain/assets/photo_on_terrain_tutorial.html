Photo on Terrain Tutorial

Drapes a single oblique aerial photo onto the actual terrain by handing four ground corners and an ImageSource to ProjectedMediaSurface. The engine's fragment shader applies a 2D homography from the four corners at every fragment, so the texture stays draped on the terrain (no 3D mesh, no intersection) and the interior is perspective-correct even for trapezoidal footprints.

This is the static counterpart of Video on Terrain, which drives the same ProjectedMediaSurface from a live KLV stream by calling setLocations once per render frame.
